Who We Are

A Tanzanian nonprofit, rooted in community.

Enyuata Maasai Experience is a newly established nonprofit organization based in Tanzania, dedicated to improving the quality of life of Maasai communities through education, women's empowerment, humanitarian assistance, and sustainable community development.

We exist to connect compassionate people, volunteers, donors, and partners from around the world with meaningful opportunities to support the Maasai community — while walking alongside local leaders, elders, and families every step of the way.

Our Vision

To be an organization that builds a prosperous Tanzania through creating communities that are healthy, knowledgeable, environmental friendly, respect and value ecological diversity.

Our Mission

To create communities that is healthy, knowledgeable and economically independent through promotion of agricultural research and eco-friendly farming system; accessible and sustainable quality health services; and access to safe water. And to have well educated, gender based violence free communities.

Our Objectives

  1. To establish healthy, knowledgeable and economically independent communities through training and aid.
  2. To promote sustainable management of natural resources so as to enhance permanent sovereignty over natural resources.
  3. To facilitate exploration of ground water, drilling of wells, installation of water pumps on drilled wells and establish water source management to poor rural and small town dwellers.
  4. To reduce maternal, newborn and children under the age of five mortality by increasing knowledge and access to preventive services.
  5. To promote sustainable agricultural enterprises and marketing through irrigation and use of new and proven sustainable technologies.
  6. To establish and promote community based co-operatives so as to enhance access to information, develop communication infrastructure and introduce new technologies in communities.
  7. To enhance social justice and conduct vocational training to young people in poor rural and small town dwellers.
  8. Sensitize the community on existence, transmission and protection of communicable diseases, Sexual Transmitted Diseases, HIV/AIDS and facilitate health education in general so that they become part in the war against such diseases.
